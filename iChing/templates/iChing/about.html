Background

The I Ching , the Book of Changes, is an ancient Chinese text with a rich history. It is tradationally used as an interpretive manual to accompany a divination process. The process always involves the translation of some real world interaction, using say yarrow stalks or coins, being converted to broken or solid lines. These lines then build up to form a set of lines with 6 lines being referred to as a hexagram. The hexagram has its own meaning, passed through the I Ching, but for those interested, additional information about the metaphorical significance of each hexagram can be gained by an understanding of its component Trigrams, of the Eight Trigrams, and their internal interactions.

The I Ching has captured the hearts and minds of many throughout history as a "foundational text for Confucian and Daoist philosophical traditions". More recently, it sparked mathematic and philosophical discussion leading to the creation of modern binary number system.

How to use this site

This site makes aims to make the I Ching accessible and easy to use. Here are the steps:

  1. Go to homepage (Click "I Ching" in the navbar)
  2. Type in your question to receive divination, and click "Consult"
  3. Toss the coins 6 times using the "Toss" button
    • Each toss will build a solid or broken line in your hexagram
    • The line will be emphasized if the toss was all heads or all tails
    • Your responding hexagram will be complete in the bottom left after 6 coin tosses
  4. Explore the response corresponding to your hexagram
  5. Provide feedback! Was the response applicable to your question? Did it help you see things in a new way?

Additionally, all 64 hexagrams are available to browse. As are records of past consultations.
